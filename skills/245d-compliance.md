# 245D Compliance Skill

## Overview

This skill provides Claude with comprehensive knowledge of Minnesota's 245D Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) regulations for effectively managing compliance within the Mercy Link portal.

## Minnesota Statutes Chapter 245D Summary

### Service Types Covered

**Community Residential Setting (CRS)** - 24-hour residential services
- Licensed capacity typically 4 individuals per home
- Requires on-site staffing coverage
- Focus areas: daily living skills, health/safety, community integration

**Individualized Community Supports (ICS)** - In-home and community support
- Flexible hours based on individual needs
- Can be provided in person's own home or community settings

**Intensive Residential Treatment Services (IRTS)** - Mental health residential
- Higher acuity mental health services
- Specific staff-to-resident ratios

### Training Hour Requirements (245D.09, Subd. 5)

**Annual training hours based on experience:**
- Less than 5 years experience: 24 hours/year
- 5+ years experience: 12 hours/year

**Required training topics:**
1. Person-centered planning
2. Positive behavior supports
3. Community integration
4. Health and safety
5. Rights of service recipients
6. Crisis intervention
7. Medication administration (if applicable)
8. Condition-specific training

### Documentation Standards

#### CSSP Addendum Must Include:
1. Individual's preferences and desired outcomes
2. Provider responsibilities
3. Specific services to be provided
4. Frequency and duration of services
5. Methods for evaluating progress
6. Individual's strengths and resources
7. Identified risks and mitigation strategies
8. Emergency backup plan
9. Signatures of all parties

#### Progress Review Must Document:
1. Progress toward outcomes
2. Effectiveness of current supports
3. Health and safety concerns
4. Need for service plan modifications
5. Incidents/changes since last review
6. Input from individual and team

### Incident Reporting Requirements (245D.06)

**Must Report Within 24 Hours:**
- Deaths
- Serious injuries requiring medical attention
- Unauthorized absence
- Any maltreatment allegation
- Emergency room visits
- Law enforcement involvement

**Report To:**
- DHS Licensing
- Case Manager/Lead Agency
- Legal Representative (if applicable)
- Guardian (if applicable)

### Abuse Prevention Plan Components (245D.071, Subd. 2)

Each individual must have a plan addressing:
1. Individual vulnerabilities
2. Supervision requirements
3. Methods to minimize risk
4. Response procedures if abuse occurs
5. Staff training requirements specific to individual
6. Environmental considerations

### Background Study Requirements (Chapter 245C)

**Who Needs Background Study:**
- All employees providing direct contact
- Contractors with direct contact
- Volunteers with regular contact

**Background Study Must Clear Before:**
- Individual begins working alone with clients
- Access to client information/records

**Disqualifying Offenses:**
- Crimes against persons
- Crimes against children
- Financial exploitation
- Maltreatment findings

## Compliance Status Definitions

**PENDING**: Due date in future, not yet completed
**COMPLETED**: Documentation uploaded, item marked complete
**OVERDUE**: Due date passed, not yet completed

## Common Compliance Issues

### Documentation Gaps
- Missing signatures on plans
- Outdated emergency contacts
- Incomplete functional assessments
- Missing person-specific training documentation

### Training Deficiencies
- Lapsed certifications (First Aid/CPR)
- Incomplete orientation within 60 days
- Missing annual training hours
- No documentation of person-specific training

### Timeline Violations
- Late CSSP Addendum development
- Missed quarterly reviews
- Late initial planning meetings
- Delayed abuse prevention plans

## Best Practices for This Portal

### Daily Operations
1. Check dashboard for overdue items each morning
2. Review "Due This Week" section
3. Verify new staff have background studies initiated

### Monthly Tasks
1. Run notification generator to update deadline warnings
2. Review training hour progress for all staff
3. Check for upcoming CSSP annual reviews

### Quarterly Tasks
1. Ensure all progress reviews are scheduled
2. Audit document completeness
3. Review and update emergency contacts

### Annual Tasks
1. Verify all staff training hours are met
2. Renew expired certifications
3. Update all CSSP Addendums
4. Complete required DHS reporting
